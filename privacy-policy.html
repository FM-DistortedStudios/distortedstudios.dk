Privacy
Policy

Last updated:  ·  Distorted Studios, Copenhagen

This policy explains how Distorted Studios ("we", "us", "our") collects, uses, and protects information when you visit our website or engage with our services. We are based in Copenhagen, Denmark, and comply with the EU General Data Protection Regulation (GDPR).
01

Who we are

Distorted Studios is a Copenhagen-based studio that builds automated revenue systems for businesses. Our website is distortedstudios.dk.

For any privacy-related matters, contact us at support@distortedstudios.dk.

02

What data we collect

We collect minimal data. The information we may collect includes:

  • Contact information — your name and email address if you reach out to us directly
  • Usage data — basic analytics such as pages visited, browser type, and general location at country level
  • Cookies — small files stored in your browser to help the site function and understand traffic patterns

We do not collect sensitive personal data and we do not run contact forms that store submissions on our servers.

03

How we use your data

Any data we collect is used solely to:

  • Respond to enquiries and communicate with potential clients
  • Understand how our website is used so we can improve it
  • Comply with legal obligations where required

We do not sell, rent, or share your data with third parties for marketing purposes.

04

Cookies

Our website may use cookies for basic analytics. These are non-intrusive and do not track you across other websites. You can disable cookies at any time through your browser settings without affecting your ability to use our site.

05

Third-party services

We may use third-party tools such as analytics platforms that have their own privacy policies. We only use services that meet GDPR standards.

Where our work involves integrations with platforms such as Pinterest, a dedicated privacy policy for those integrations is available at distortedstudios.dk/pinterest-privacy.html.

06

Your rights under GDPR

As a resident of the EU/EEA you have the right to:

  • Access the personal data we hold about you
  • Request correction or deletion of your data
  • Object to or restrict how we process your data
  • Request a portable copy of your data
  • Withdraw consent at any time where processing is based on consent

To exercise any of these rights, contact us and we will respond within 30 days.

07

Data retention

We retain personal data only for as long as necessary to fulfil the purpose it was collected for, or as required by law. Email correspondence is kept for a maximum of 2 years unless an ongoing business relationship requires otherwise.

08

Changes to this policy

We may update this policy from time to time. Any changes will be reflected with an updated date at the top of this page.
